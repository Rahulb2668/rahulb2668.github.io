Components

DC motor

The motor used to control the motion of the tray and consequently the door is a 12v 100 rpm Johnson DC motor. Sourced from here(Robu.in). Its connected to the Tray via a belt driven system. Its far pulley is coupled together with an Encoder encoder.

Encoder

A two channel Opto-Electrical incremental encoder with 50 PPR, for position feedback.They can be found here(Robu)

Time Of Flight

A IR based device used to measure the distance within the box to decide “Presence” or “Absence” of food.(Inscribe)

Hall Effect Sensor

The end positions of tray is fixed and indicated with the help of two limit switches. Provides logical stops in the code. (Inscribe)

Solenoid Lock

A 12v electro-mechanical solenoid switch. When triggered by supplying 12v it “unlock” cause the stopper to be retracted allowing for the door be opened. Its an active device that only works when power is supplied, it is hence reset when power is turned off. It can be found here (Robu)

LED strip

A 12v RGB 4 line LED strip. Seperate lines of Red, Blue and Green and supply voltage. It is connected in the common anode configuration.

Temperature Sensor

DS18B20 digital thermometer which runs on 1-wire protocol. Used for reading the temperature and in control of the Heater PID. It can be found here. Here is the Datasheet

Heater

connections

A coil based AC heater , used to heat the surrounding air and then circulated using the Fan .

Warning
Never run the heater without the fan. As it causes the heater to rapidly heat up.

Fan

AC cross-flow fan, a squirrel cage motor used for circulating the heated air within the box.(TechnoTechnologies)

Note
The Fan needs minimum power to run will not run below 50% of full Power.

Motor Driver

DRV8701 Full bridge motor driver, for the motor speed and direction Control. (Inscribe) Datasheet

RS485 Communication

The communication between the master and the slave happens using the UART protocol over the RS485 electrical interface.(Inscribe)

Triac

AC control for the heater and fan. Two channel one for the heater and the other for the fan. Both are controlled based on a PWM using a fixed 100Hz timer ( 50Hz * 2 ) and turned off using the ZC (zero crossing) interrupt.

ULN2003

A dip type IC used as a Digital switch to control the 3 channels of the LED and the solenoid.

Connections and Jacks

  • RJ45 cable : Used as the serial bus and also carries 12v supply for all the Motherboard (regulated to 3.3V). Generally an 8 wire cable, converted to a 6 wire cable by shortting Vcc and GND to 1 additional port each. Other 4 is used to carry the differential communication signal from the RS485 (A,B,Z,Y)
  • DC jack : Used for the raw 12v supply for the motors, solenoid and LEDs.
  • AC connector : 3 pin connector for the AC supply (live, earth and neutral) used to power the Triac and in turn the Fan and Heater
